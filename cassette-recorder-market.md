<p><p>Cassette recorders, popular in the 80s, used two main market types of cassettes: Type I and Type II. Type I cassettes were the standard, offering good sound quality and durability for everyday use. On the other hand, Type II cassettes, also known as chrome cassettes, were designed for high-quality audio recordings. They had improved frequency response and a higher signal-to-noise ratio, making them ideal for professional or audiophile use. Both types catered to different consumer needs within the cassette recorder market.</p></p>
