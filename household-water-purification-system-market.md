<p><ul><li>Activated Carbon Filter Media</li><li>Ion Exchange Resin Media</li><li>Reverse Osmosis Membrane</li><li>UV Disinfection</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Household water purification systems come in various types to cater to different needs and preferences. Activated carbon filter media is effective at removing organic compounds and chlorine from water. Ion exchange resin media helps in softening hard water by removing calcium and magnesium ions. Reverse osmosis membrane technology removes impurities through a semi-permeable membrane. UV disinfection uses ultraviolet light to kill bacteria and viruses in water. Other types of purification systems include distillation, ceramic filters, and ozonation. Each type offers unique advantages in ensuring clean and safe drinking water for households.</p></p>

<p><p>Household water purification systems, including point-of-use (POU) and point-of-entry (POE) systems, are crucial for ensuring clean and safe drinking water in homes. POU systems are typically installed at individual taps or sinks to treat water at the point of consumption, while POE systems treat water as it enters the home, providing comprehensive whole-house water filtration. These systems remove contaminants and impurities, improving water quality and protecting the health of household members. The market for these systems continues to grow as water quality concerns rise.</p></p>
