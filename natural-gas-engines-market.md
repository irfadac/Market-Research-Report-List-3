<p><p>External-mix Natural Gas Engines incorporate a separate mixer to mix the fuel with air outside the combustion chamber. These engines are typically used in light-duty vehicles and small equipment. On the other hand, Internal-mix Natural Gas Engines blend the fuel and air inside the combustion chamber before ignition. These engines are more commonly found in heavy-duty vehicles and industrial equipment. Both types of engines have their own advantages and applications depending on the specific requirements of the user.</p></p>
