<p><p>Microfluidic chips are used for precise manipulation of small fluid volumes in various applications such as healthcare, biotechnology, and research. These chips can be classified into three main types based on the materials they are made of: polymers, ceramics, and semi-conductors. Polymers are cost-effective and flexible, ceramics offer high chemical and temperature resistance, while semi-conductors provide precise control and integration with electronic components. Each type of microfluidic chip has its own unique advantages and is chosen based on the specific requirements of the application.</p></p>
