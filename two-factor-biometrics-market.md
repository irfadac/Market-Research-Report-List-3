<p><p>Passwords and hard tokens are considered traditional forms of two-factor authentication in the biometrics market. Passwords involve something the user knows, while hard tokens are physical devices that the user possesses. Soft tokens and OTP (One-Time Passwords) are more modern forms, typically generated through apps or messages. Biometrics, however, are the most advanced form, involving unique biological characteristics like fingerprints or facial recognition. These types of biometrics provide a high level of security and convenience for users.</p></p>
