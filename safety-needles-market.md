<p><p>Active safety needles have a mechanism that requires the healthcare worker to activate it in order for the needle to retract or be covered after use, reducing the risk of needlestick injuries. Passive safety needles, on the other hand, have built-in safety features that automatically activate after use without requiring any additional steps from the healthcare worker. Both types of safety needles are designed to protect healthcare workers from accidental needlestick injuries while administering injections or drawing blood.</p></p>
