<p><p>Compartment interior coatings are divided into two main types: Basecoat and Topcoat. Basecoat is the initial layer of coating applied to the surface of the compartment, providing adhesion and corrosion resistance. Topcoat is the final layer that adds aesthetics, durability, and weather resistance to the compartment interior. Basecoat serves as the foundation for the coating system, while Topcoat gives the final finish and protection. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing the appearance and longevity of compartment interiors.</p></p>
