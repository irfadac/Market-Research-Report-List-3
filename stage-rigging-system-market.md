<p><p>Stage rigging systems can be broadly classified into two categories: manual and automatic. Manual rigging systems require human operators to physically adjust the rigging equipment, such as ropes and pulleys, to raise and lower stage elements. On the other hand, automatic rigging systems utilize motorized components that can be controlled remotely to automate the lifting and lowering of stage equipment. Both types of rigging systems have their own advantages and are used in different settings based on the specific requirements of the event or performance.</p></p>
