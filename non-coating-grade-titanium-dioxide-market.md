<p><p>Non-coating grade titanium dioxide is commonly produced through two main processes: sulfate and chloride process. The sulfate process involves the reaction of titanium ores with sulfuric acid, producing a lower quality but more cost-effective titanium dioxide. On the other hand, the chloride process involves the reaction of titanium tetrachloride with oxygen, resulting in a higher quality and more pure titanium dioxide. Both processes cater to different market needs based on their specific properties and applications.</p></p>
