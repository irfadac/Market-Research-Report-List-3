<p><p>Dental gypsum is a material commonly used in dentistry for making dental molds and casts. It is a white powder that is mixed with water to create a thick paste, which can then be poured into a mold to create a replica of a patient's teeth or gums. The resulting cast is used by dentists to create custom dental appliances such as crowns, bridges, and dentures.</p><p>The global dental gypsum market is experiencing significant growth, with an expected CAGR of 13.7% during the forecast period.
