<p><ul><li>Monoclonal Antibodies (MABs)</li><li>Cancer Vaccines</li><li>Immunomodulators</li><li>Adoptive Cell transfer</li><li>Checkpoint Inhibitors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cancer immunotherapies utilize the body's immune system to fight cancer cells. Monoclonal Antibodies (MABs) target specific proteins on cancer cells to block their growth. Cancer vaccines stimulate the immune system to recognize and destroy cancer cells. Immunomodulators enhance the immune response against cancer. Adoptive Cell transfer involves transferring immune cells to target and kill cancer cells. Checkpoint Inhibitors block proteins that prevent immune cells from attacking cancer. Each type of immunotherapy works in a unique way to treat cancer.</p></p>
