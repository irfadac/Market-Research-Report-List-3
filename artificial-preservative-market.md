<p><p>Artificial preservatives are commonly categorized into different types based on their chemical composition and function. Sorbates, such as potassium sorbate, are commonly used to prevent mold and yeast growth. Benzoates, like sodium benzoate, are effective against bacteria and fungi. Propionates, such as calcium propionate, are mainly used for preventing mold in bread products. Nitrites are used to prevent bacterial growth in processed meats. Sulfates are commonly used in dried fruits and vegetables. Other types of preservatives may include antioxidants like BHA and BHT.</p></p>

<p><p>The artificial preservative market applications can be categorized into four main segments: antimicrobial, antioxidant, chelator, and others. Antimicrobial preservatives are used to inhibit the growth of bacteria, fungi, and other microorganisms in food and beverage products. Antioxidant preservatives are added to prevent oxidation reactions, which can cause food spoilage. Chelator preservatives are used to bind metal ions in food products, preventing oxidation and degradation. Other preservatives may include pH regulators, color stabilizers, and flavor enhancers to extend the shelf life of products.</p></p>
